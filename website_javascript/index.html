Geologic field data:

Outcrop mapped along buffalo bayou:

COLOR: The brown lines are lineations mapped from outcrop observations and LIDAR. White lines are possible outcrops.

SOURCE: This data was collected with the Midland Valley Clino structure geology app. All locations are preliminary. Jerry Kendall

LIMITATIONS: The brown lines on the top map (from the KMZ file) are all topographic alignments ( lineaments), only 2 of which are associated with known active faults. This dataset is originally in KMZ form, which a zip file format that must be unzipped to be used, so it can take a few seconds to appear on the page.

Regional fault mapping:

COLOR: Regional faults are in black.

LIMITATIONS: These are mapped from subsurface seismic imagery. They are mostly not exposed in surface. Geographic position should be interpreted as very approximate give they are not exposed at surface. They are shown here to indicate regional patterns of structural features.

For non-geologists

You might be surprised by all the linear features that may be faults in places. The brown lines on the top map (from the KMZ file) are all topographic alignments ( lineaments), only 2 of which are associated with known active faults. First, the faults that exist in this part of the gulf coast are slow gradual moving growth faults rather than the types of faults in other part of the world that produce earthquakes. Second, not all of the linear features are faults. Some are linear features that might be things other than faults. A map of active surface faults in the Houston area from can be found at this web map from the USGS publication Sachin D Shah & Jennifer Lanning Rush, 2005. However, even these move very slowly and it is only the most active parts of the faults that cause problems in the way of cracks to foundations.
